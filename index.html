Requesting items

  • Rules

Books on loan from the Library's standard and 1 week loan collections can be requested using the QCat Library Catalogue.

  • Search for the item in the QCat Library Catalogue
  • A 'Request it' button is displayed if all requestable copies are on loan
  • Click on the link to place your request
  • If you haven't already logged in to My Account, you will need to enter your Library / Student number and password
  • Select where you would like to collect the item when it is available (pick up location) and click to confirm your request

When a copy is available for you to collect you will be notified by email.

You can also check your account on Queen's Online or the QCat Library Catalogue to see if your requests are available.

Books and journal articles can be requested from other Queen's libraries if they are not available in the library you are using.

Requests are made via the Inter-Branch Loan service. Staff at any of the Borrower Services Desks can take your request.

Requested items can be picked up from the following areas:

  • Self-service collection shelves in The McClay Library and Biomedical Library.
  • Borrower Services Desk in the Medical Library or AFBI libraries.

Please cancel your request if you no longer need a book - other people might be waiting for it.

We operate a request system so that we can make popular items available to as many borrowers as possible.

  • You can reserve up to 5 items
  • You can reserve standard loan items and 1 week loan items
  • You cannot reserve 1 night, 2 night or 7 night loan items

Borrowing from the library

You can access the (QCat) Library catalogue and most electronic resources from any PC on the University campus.

You can access library electronic resources anywhere, through Queen’s Online.

Use the QCat Library Catalogue to find out if the Library holds an item you require.

  • You can search by author, title or keyword.
  • If we have the item you are looking for it will be displayed, along with details of the Library in which it is held, how long it can be borrowed for and its library shelfmark location.
  • Books, journals and other resources are arranged in shelfmark order.
  • The shelfmark is the combination of letters and numbers printed on the spine (and/or front cover) of each item.
  • Everything in the Library is shelved alphabetically and numerically.
  • If you can't find a book contact the nearest Borrower Services or Enquiry Desk.
  • For books / journal articles that you need that are not available in Queen’s see Inter-Library Loans (ILL).
  • Remember, much of the content of the library's journal collections can be found online together with a growing collection of ebooks.
